📖 About Each Stage

🔄 Perimenopause

Typical age: 40s to early 50s

Hormone levels begin to fluctuate. Periods may become irregular. This phase can last 4–10 years.

  • 🔸 Hot flashes & night sweats
  • 🔸 Mood swings & irritability
  • 🔸 Sleep disturbances
  • 🔸 Weight gain, especially around the belly
  • Recommendation: Start isometric exercises, track symptoms, prioritize sleep

⏸ Menopause

Defined as: 12 consecutive months without a period

Estrogen levels drop significantly. Symptoms may intensify before gradually improving.

  • 🔸 Increased belly fat storage
  • 🔸 Vaginal dryness
  • 🔸 Decreased muscle mass
  • 🔸 Brain fog & memory lapses
  • Recommendation: Consistent exercise routine, strength training, hydration

🌺 Post-Menopause

Timing: After 12+ months without a period, for the rest of life

Hormone levels stabilize at a new low. Many symptoms ease, but health risks change.

  • 🔸 Higher risk of osteoporosis
  • 🔸 Increased cardiovascular risk
  • 🔸 Slower metabolism
  • 🔸 Joint pain & stiffness
  • Recommendation: Weight-bearing exercise, calcium & vitamin D, regular check-ups
