1. The Front Plank

Transverse abdominis, shoulders, glutes

Why it works: The foundational isometric. Forces your entire core to engage continuously. No crunch compares.

  1. Start on forearms, elbows under shoulders
  2. Step feet back, body in straight line from heels to head
  3. Squeeze glutes, pull belly button toward spine
  4. Hold 20-30 seconds. Breathe.

2. The Dead Bug Hold

Transverse abdominis, pelvic floor, deep spinal stabilizers

Why it works: Directly trains the deep core and pelvic floor — areas weakened by menopause. Teaches your core to stabilize your spine.

  1. Lie on back, arms toward ceiling, legs in tabletop position
  2. Press lower back firmly into the floor
  3. Hold and breathe deeply
  4. Start with 20 seconds

3. The Glute Bridge Hold

Glutes, hamstrings, lower back, transverse abdominis

Why it works: Activates the largest muscle in your body — the glutes — which boosts metabolism and improves posture instantly.

  1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat on floor hip-width apart
  2. Lift hips until body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees
  3. Squeeze glutes at the top, hold 30-45 seconds

4. The Wall Sit

Quadriceps, glutes, core stabilizers

Why it works: Builds leg strength and core stability simultaneously. The longer you hold, the more your deep core fires.

  1. Stand with back against a wall, feet 2 feet from wall
  2. Slide down until thighs are parallel to floor
  3. Hold 20-30 seconds. Build up to 60 seconds.

5. The Bird Dog Hold

Erector spinae, transverse abdominis, glutes, shoulders

Why it works: Trains your deep core to stabilize against movement — exactly what flattens the belly in daily life.

  1. Start on hands and knees, neutral spine
  2. Extend right arm forward and left leg back simultaneously
  3. Hold 15-20 seconds. Switch sides.
